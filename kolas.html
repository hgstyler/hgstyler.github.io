Environmental Proficiency Testing Promotion Roadmap

An innovative journey to restore reliability of Korea's environmental data and secure global competitiveness.

Background & Necessity

Ensuring data reliability is essential for sustainable environmental policies. Accurate measurement data of environmental pollutants such as air, water, and odor are crucial for developing appropriate countermeasures. However, the environmental testing and analysis sector in South Korea has been limited to perfunctory quality control that does not meet international standards, raising doubts about the reliability of measurement results.

In 2019, some environmental measurement agencies were caught issuing fraudulent reports without actual measurements, becoming a social issue. Although the government has since supplemented the system, most laboratories still receive "99% suitable" ratings, indicating overly lenient evaluations. Without adopting a strict proficiency testing system based on international standards, these reliability issues are bound to continue.

To solve this problem, the establishment of a private proficiency testing institution compliant with international standards (ISO/IEC 17043) is urgent. In advanced countries, privately-led proficiency testing is active, and numerous laboratories verify their analytical capabilities through it. For example, ERA (Waters ERA) in the United States has grown into a world-leading proficiency test provider trusted by thousands of laboratories in over 80 countries, offering products and services with international accreditations such as ISO 17043 and NELAC. In Korea, institutions like the Korea Conformity Laboratories (KCL) have also begun operating programs after being accredited by KOLAS as an ISO/IEC 17043 accredited proficiency testing provider. Recently, Korea Water Resources Corporation (K-water) also obtained international accreditation as a proficiency testing provider in the water environment sector, indicating a shift.

Our Strengths

Our team aims to establish South Korea's first specialized environmental proficiency testing institution, aligning with this trend. Our expert personnel, prepared over the past five years, possess experience in operating internationally accredited testing laboratories (KOLAS ISO/IEC 17025) and have completed ISO/IEC 17043 practical training. Furthermore, we hold a domestic patent for a field air proficiency testing method.

This patent is an innovative technology with international utility and will serve as a foundation for global market entry through future PCT international patent applications.

In summary, establishing a privately-led international standard proficiency testing system is necessary to restore the reliability of South Korea's environmental data and secure international competitiveness. Our goal is to pursue this in conjunction with national policies and, in the long term, grow into the top reliability verification institution in the global environmental sector.

Promotion Roadmap

Phase 1: Government Proposal & Policy Adoption Strategy

First, we must propose our business item to the highest decision-making levels of the government to persuade them to adopt it as a national task. To achieve this, we will approach through multiple channels simultaneously, including the Presidential Office, the Prime Minister's Office, and the National Assembly's Environment and Labor Committee. The specific implementation plans are as follows:

  • Utilize Official Presidential Office Channels: Submit proposals through the 'National Mailbox' operated by the Presidential Office. Emphasize that our proposal is a timely policy suggestion related to national safety and global environmental leadership.
  • Select Proposal Timing to Align with Government Schedule: To increase the success rate of the proposal, it is crucial to target periods when the government's attention is focused on environmental issues. For instance, around World Environment Day (June 5) or just before the UN Climate Change Conference (COP).
  • Proposal Content & Recipient Strategy: Simultaneously submit proposals to the Prime Minister's Office's public communication channels (e.g., National Sinmungo proposal section) and send proposals or request meetings with the Chairperson and floor leaders of the National Assembly's Environment and Labor Committee.
  • Induce Government Response to Promotion Content: After submitting the proposal, monitor its review and response. If positive signals (e.g., contact from the Presidential Secretariat or Prime Minister's Office policy officer) are received, immediately suggest forming a response team (TF) and propose our participation.

Phase 2: Public Relations & Opinion Formation Strategy

If the government's response is insufficient or rejected, indirect pressure for policy change must be applied through social issue-raising. However, since proficiency testing is a somewhat unfamiliar and technical topic to the general public, targeted public relations emphasizing professionalism and public interest will be more effective. The following approaches can be considered:

  • Utilize Environmental Professional Media & Content: Before direct public promotion, inform opinion leaders in the environmental sector. For example, report to KBS's environmental documentary channel "Environmental Special" to have it covered as content.
  • Collaborate with Environmental NGOs & Expert Networks: Contact major environmental NGOs such as the Korea Federation for Environmental Movement (KFEM) and Green Korea United to cooperate on issue-raising. Propose joint statements or press conferences to highlight the seriousness of the problem through expert voices.
  • Connect with Media Reporting & Investigative Journalism: Since reliability issues are directly linked to public safety, they are valuable items for investigative reporting by major media outlets. Based on the reports and data prepared by our team, report to environmental journalists. The key is to sensationalize the issue at the public's eye level, such as "Shocking Reality of Certified Test Reports."
  • Online Campaigns (Secondary/Auxiliary): In addition to issue-raising through professional channels, online national petitions or SNS campaigns can be conducted. Utilize the National Assembly's national consent petition system or platforms like Change.org to post online petitions.

Phase 3: Business Promotion & Global Expansion Roadmap

If an opportunity for policy adoption arises through public opinion or internal government review, it is now time to actually implement the business and generate results. The best-case scenario is for the government to accept our proposal and form a joint TFT (Task Force), while the alternative is to proceed independently without government support but actively attract external resources. We prepare for both cases with the following roadmap:

  • If Government Joins: National Project: If working with the government (Ministry of Environment or Presidential direct TF), focus on establishing an institutional foundation. Form a TF with relevant ministry officials, our team's experts, and external experts to establish detailed implementation plans.
  • If Private Independent Promotion: Phased Growth Strategy: Even if direct government participation or budget support is not obtained, we already have secured technology and personnel, so we will proceed with commercialization through private channels. We will actively utilize government startup support programs and attract investment.
  • Domestic Foundation Building (2025~2026): From the second half of 2025 to 2026, we will focus on strengthening our domestic foundation. Complete KOLAS accreditation preparations to officially be recognized as a proficiency testing provider within 2026.
  • International Expansion Preparation (2026~2027): From late 2026, once a certain number of domestic participating institutions are secured and accredited status is obtained, we will begin pioneering the international market. First, proceed with PCT international application for our patented technology to protect intellectual property rights.

In summary, in Phase 3, we will mobilize all available resources, whether governmental or private, to execute the business, create successful cases domestically, and then use this as a stepping stone for internationalization. Throughout this process, we will continuously promote achievements and, if legal and institutional improvements are needed, propose them to the government for support. Ultimately, we will steadily advance step by step to achieve our business vision: "South Korea's leading international standard environmental proficiency testing institution, a guardian of data quality trusted by the world."
